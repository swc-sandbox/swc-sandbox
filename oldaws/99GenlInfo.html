General Information

  • Title: “Local-to-Global Principles in Arithmetical Algebraic Geometry”
  • Dates: March 13-17, 1999
  • Location: The University of Arizona
  • Organizers: William McCallum, Wayne Raskind, and Douglas Ulmer
  • The conference poster: front (243KB) and back (193KB)

Speakers and Courses

(The links below lead to the corresponding sections of the notes page.)
  • Jean-Louis Colliot-Thélène, assisted by David Harari and Alexei Skorobogatov: “The Hasse principle”
  • Barry Mazur: “Visualizing elements of the Shafarevich-Tate group”(One lecture)
  • Bill McCallum, assisted by Pavlos Tzermias and Joseph Wetherell: “The method of Coleman and Chabauty”
  • Bjorn Poonen: “Mordell-Weil groups, Selmer groups, and Shafarevich-Tate groups”
  • Karl Rubin: “Shafarevich-Tate groups of CM and modular elliptic curves”

Professional Development Component

  • The focus of the professional development component was on symbolic computation. John Cremona spoke on “Computational aspects of 2-descents on elliptic curves” and gave a “Survey of computer algebra systems”.
