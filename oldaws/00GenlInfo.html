General Information

  • Title: “Topics in the Arithmetic of Function Fields”
  • Dates: March 11-15, 2000
  • Location: The University of Arizona
  • Organizers: Dinesh Thakur, Douglas Ulmer, and Felipe Voloch

Speakers and Courses

(The links below lead to the corresponding sections of the notes page.)
  • Greg Anderson: “Introduction to fermionic Fock space for number theorists”
  • Nicholas Katz: “Four lectures on Weil II”
  • Kristin Lauter, René Schoof, and Michael Zieve: “The number of points on a curve over a finite field”
  • Peter Sarnak: “Zeta functions and symmetry” (One lecture)
  • Douglas Ulmer: “Elliptic curves over function fields and a Gross-Zagier theorem”

Professional Development Component

  • The focus of the professional development component was on the job market, both inside and outside academia. Jeffrey Lagarias of AT&T Research spoke on Mathematics in Industry, and there was a panel discussion on the experiences of some mathematicians in non-academic jobs. The panelists were Jeffrey Lagarias (AT&T Research), Kristin Lauter (Microsoft), Charles Toll (NSA), and Michael Zieve (IDA).
